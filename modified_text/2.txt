This report examines the tactics of a hostile group known as "Adversary Bravo," which came to attention in May 2015. Adversary Bravo is infamous for its email schemes that deceive recipients into activating malicious software. Their technique involves masking dangerous software as harmless files, such as "resume.pdf," and altering system settings to secure ongoing access and control. The malicious program, referred to as "Poison Ivy Variant d1c6," is particularly stealthy, with distinct identifiers in its files and the system changes it triggers. This analysis highlights the systematic approach used by Adversary Bravo to deploy these deceptive methods, underlining the importance of continual vigilance to protect against such sophisticated cyber threats.
